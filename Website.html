About the Project

About SIM

A student-built platform designed to make campus safety simple, visible, and collaborative.

What is SIM?

Mapping safety, one report at a time

The Security Incident Mapping System (SIM) is a campus safety platform that lets students, staff, and visitors report security incidents in a simple, structured way. Reports are visualised on a campus map so the security team can respond quickly and spot recurring patterns.

SIM was developed as a Software Engineering (SEN202) project to demonstrate how thoughtful interface design can encourage community participation in campus safety.

Project Objectives

What we set out to achieve

  • Provide a fast, friendly way to report campus incidents.
  • Improve communication between students and the security office.
  • Raise security awareness through regular safety updates.
  • Help the security team identify incident hotspots on campus.
Benefits

Why SIM matters

When reporting is easy, more people report — and when more people report, campus becomes measurably safer. SIM lowers the barrier to speaking up while giving the security team clear, organised information they can act on.

  • Faster response times to incidents.
  • A more informed and alert campus community.
  • Data-driven patrol planning and prevention.
